Albert Einstein

A tribute to the father of modern physics

Biography

Albert Einstein was a theoretical physicist who developed the theory of relativity, one of the two pillars of modern physics. His work is also known for its influence on the philosophy of science. He received the Nobel Prize in Physics in 1921 for his discovery of the photoelectric effect, which was pivotal in establishing quantum theory.
